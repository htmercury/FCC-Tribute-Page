Club Penguin

The social network for kids and teens (2005 - 2017)

Club Penguin was an online-based, massively multiplayer online role-playing game (abbreviated as a MMORPG) created by Disney Canada Inc. (previously New Horizons Interactive) in the summer of 2005. It involved a virtual world, where the players were penguins, and was featuring a large range of minigames and other online activities, and was based on a snowy island, after which the game was named.

Here's a timeline of Club Penguin's Existence:

  • Late 2004 - Rsnail came up with the idea for Club Penguin one day in 2004 while watching the news.
  • Mid 2005 - The developers financed their Club Penguin start-up entirely with their own credit cards and personal lines of credit, and maintained 100 percent ownership.
  • August 2005 - Beta website launches, where people could beta test the game and search for bugs and glitches.
  • October 2005 - Game is officially released in English managed by only 10 employees and 15,000 of penguins signed up the same day.
  • March 2005 - Club Penguin has over 1.4 million user accounts.
  • Late 2006 - Club Penguin had reached hundreds of employees by late 2006, and it soon became one of the most popular virtual worlds around.
  • August 2007 - Club Penguin gets bought by Disney for $350 million.
  • Late 2007 - Club Penguin has over 30 million user accounts.
  • October 2008 - The first plush and figurine toys were released. There was a big party in Times Square in New York City to celebrate three years of Club Penguin.
  • Early 2010 - Both Rsnail and Screenhog left the game. Rsnail left Club Penguin to pursue a new project, Mech Mice.
  • October 2012 - Billybob left the game to create new educational technology for schools.
  • June 2013 - Club Penguin creates their own official Facebook page.
  • July 2013 - Club Penguin has over 200 million registered user accounts.
  • April 2015 - It was revealed that Disney Interactive had laid off 28 members of Club Penguin's Kelowna headquarters due to the game's declining popularity.
  • January 2017 - It was announced that the game would be discontinued on March 29, 2017
  • March 2017 - Club Penguin later shut down its servers on March 30, 2017 at 12:01 AM. The game was replaced by a successor, titled Club Penguin Island.
